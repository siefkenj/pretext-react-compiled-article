Then use the names of these locations for the placement of labels. A large filled circle is a good shape to use. A radius that will scale to a radius of about 3 millimeters is a good choice. You will want to move your label away from the shape so there is no overlap, and thus the radius of your circle would be a good offset. The label can move in one of eight directions: south, southwest, west, etc. Try to arrange the direction of your label so that it does not overwrite other graphics elements of your diagramâ€”this will be confusing to a blind reader. So an example (spread over two lines) is
<label xml:id="pos-y-axis" location="positive-y-axis"
direction="northeast" offset="3mm"><m>y</m>-axis</label>
The @xml:id is not strictly necessary, but can be useful for debugging.
