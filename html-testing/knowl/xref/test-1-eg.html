Example 1.0.2.

Find the derivatives of the following functions:
  1. \(y = \sin(2x)\text{.}\)
  2. \(y= \ln(4x^3-2x^2)\text{.}\)
  3. \(y = e^{-x^2}\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. Recognize that \(y = e^{-x^2}\) is the composition of \(f(x) = e^x\) and \(g(x) = -x^2\text{.}\) Remembering that \(\fp(x) = e^x\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} y' \amp = e^{-x^2}\cdot \frac{d}{dx}(-x^2)\\ \amp = e^{-x^2}\cdot (-2x)\\ \amp = -2xe^{-x^2}\text{.} \end{align*}
